Towards Social Media Analytics

Author: Junru Zhong Date: January 20, 2020  22:01:37 Category: Sociology

We are moving our paces to analyzing social media.

I got this title from a Medium blog I recently refer to: Towards Data Science. Personally, I began to pay attention to society many years ago, and I am definitely a member of the generation of social media.

To know what is “social media analytics”, let’s see these separate concepts first.

We human beings are social animal. A society can be considered as a group of human beings (or other kind of animal), with different jobs and roles assigned to individuals. As individuals, we connect to other individuals, form groups, create products.

Long before the social media can exist, people already connect to each other by multiple media. Move back to the ancient time, our ancestors, invented languages and characters, which can be considered as methods to encode messages in today’s perspective. Then later in the 16th century, the new era of sailing connected the people around the globe, and we started to use electricity to transmit messages from 19th century.

Society is developing as the technologies develop. Social media is an expand of the real-world society in cyberspace. It is the combination of language, photography, information technology, and other technologies. We won’t have social media if we don’t have any of the technologies of encoding images, videos, text on the cyberspace, and the technology of building the Internet.

On social media, the information produce by the enormous of users was well encoded, stored, and transmitted on the cyberspace. It has never be more easier than now to collecting information produce by individuals than on social media.

We always want to create values from information. As we can collect data easily from social media, methods and applications on analyzing information, or data from social media emerge. We do social media analytics to interpret the data, and make the data meaningful. Furthermore, we can produce values from the data by analytics.

As we can find texts, photos, audios, and videos on modern social network, the analytics methods of social network analytics are always involve related techniques in analyzing those kind of data. And the Natural Language Processing, or NLP, is the most commonly used set of methods in social network analytics.

I took part in the competition team at BNU-HKBU United International College on SemEval 2019 Task 6 [2] last year. This task was about predicting offensive language from tweets. As it was done around October 2018, we made use of the latest revolutionary language model, the BERT from Google [3] to encode and classify the tweets. Finally, we achieved a very good performance, and the paper was published on NAACL 2019 [4].

We can still have chances to do more on social network analytics that can help the society to be better. And we don’t know, how will the new technologies like the fifth generation mobile communication network boost the future society. Maybe we can create new kinds of social media, new form of information or data, but we can always unearth the values hidden inside the data. That’s always true for any analytics.
